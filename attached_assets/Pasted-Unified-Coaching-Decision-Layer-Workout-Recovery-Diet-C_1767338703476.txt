Unified Coaching Decision Layer (Workout · Recovery · Diet Coherence)

Objective
Enforce a single, unified coaching decision layer where workout, recovery, and diet are interdependent, and changes in one domain automatically trigger evaluation (and adjustment if relevant) in the others — but only when justified.

⸻

1. Single Coaching Decision Layer (Hard Rule)
	•	All coaching decisions must pass through a single decision context, not separate logic for:
	•	Workouts
	•	Recovery
	•	Diet
	•	The Trainer must reason in this order:
	1.	Recovery state
	2.	Training load & progression
	3.	Nutritional support

No domain may be updated in isolation.

⸻

2. Dependency Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Recovery → Workout & Diet
	•	If recovery state is:
	•	reduce_volume or deload_suggested:
	•	Workout intensity/volume must be reduced
	•	Diet calories must be:
	•	Maintained or slightly reduced (never increased)
	•	Protein preserved
	•	Trainer must explain the adjustment once, then ask for confirmation.

⸻

Workout → Diet
	•	If training load is:
	•	Increased (volume, intensity, frequency):
	•	Diet calories must be evaluated
	•	Increase calories only if recovery supports it
	•	If training load is:
	•	Reduced or paused:
	•	Diet calories may be adjusted downward
	•	Only if reduction is expected to persist

⸻

Diet → Workout & Recovery
	•	If calories are:
	•	Reduced significantly:
	•	Trainer must reassess training intensity
	•	Prevent overload while under-fueled
	•	Protein targets must never be reduced due to recovery logic alone.

⸻

3. Relevance Filter (Prevents Overreaction)

Before applying any cross-domain change, the Trainer must check:
	•	Is the signal:
	•	Acute (1 bad day) → do nothing
	•	Persistent (trend) → adjust
	•	Does the change:
	•	Improve outcomes?
	•	Reduce risk?

If no clear benefit, the Trainer must leave other domains unchanged.

⸻

4. Mandatory Explanation & Confirmation

When a change affects multiple domains:
	1.	Trainer issues a single verdict explaining:
	•	What changed
	•	Why it changed
	•	What else was adjusted as a result
	2.	Trainer asks:
“Do you want me to apply these changes?”
	3.	Only after confirmation:
	•	Persist workout updates
	•	Persist diet updates
	•	Update recovery expectations
	•	Reflect changes across tabs

No partial updates.

⸻

5. Display Rules (Tabs)
	•	Trainer (Chat):
	•	Decision + explanation + confirmation
	•	Diet Tab:
	•	Shows final calorie & macro targets only
	•	Activities / Plans:
	•	Show final confirmed workouts only
	•	Recovery indicators:
	•	Inform decisions but do not independently modify plans

Tabs never initiate changes.

⸻

6. Explicit Safeguards
	•	No automatic calorie changes without workout context
	•	No workout progression without recovery clearance
	•	No recovery-based advice without training implications
	•	No silent background adjustments

⸻

Governing Law (Lock This)

NutriCore does not optimize parts — it optimizes the system.

Workout, recovery, and diet must always move together when relevant, and remain unchanged when not.

⸻

Acceptance Criteria
	•	Users never see conflicting advice
	•	Changes feel intentional, not reactive
	•	Trainer feels like a real coach managing trade-offs
	•	No domain drifts independently
