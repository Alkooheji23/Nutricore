🔒 Prompt 5 — Trainer Authority & Unified Decision Layer (NutriCore)

Objective
Establish the Trainer as the single decision authority across training, recovery, diet, and activity, ensuring no part of the system operates independently or contradicts another.

⸻

1️⃣ Single Brain Rule (Non-Negotiable)
	•	The Trainer is the only entity allowed to:
	•	Adjust workout plans
	•	Adjust calorie targets
	•	Interpret recovery and readiness
	•	No tab, feature, or integration may make autonomous decisions.

All other sections are execution or display layers only.

⸻

2️⃣ Unified Decision Layer

All decisions must consider together:
	•	Training load & performance
	•	Recovery signals (sleep, HRV, fatigue)
	•	Activity level (steps, cardio)
	•	Diet adherence and calorie targets
	•	User’s primary goal

No domain may be adjusted in isolation.

Example:
	•	If training volume increases → recovery & diet must be evaluated
	•	If recovery is compromised → training and diet must adapt
	•	If calories are reduced → training stress must be reassessed

⸻

3️⃣ Decision Application Rules
	•	Decisions are made on a weekly cadence (per Prompt 3)
	•	Changes apply forward only
	•	No retroactive edits
	•	No silent mid-week overhauls unless injury or illness is detected

⸻

4️⃣ Data Awareness Requirement
	•	The Trainer must always read from:
	•	Logged workouts (live or post-workout)
	•	Synced smartwatch data (steps, calories, sleep, HRV)
	•	Progress metrics (weight, trends)
	•	The Trainer never asks for data that already exists
	•	Missing data defaults to conservative assumptions

⸻

5️⃣ User Interaction Boundary
	•	Trainer:
	•	Issues decisions
	•	Explains only when asked
	•	User:
	•	Executes plans
	•	Logs data
	•	System:
	•	Enforces coherence

No debates. No parallel logic. No contradictions.

⸻

Governing Principle

One Trainer.
One Decision Layer.
One Coherent System.

⸻

This prompt locks NutriCore into a true coaching system, not a collection of features.
