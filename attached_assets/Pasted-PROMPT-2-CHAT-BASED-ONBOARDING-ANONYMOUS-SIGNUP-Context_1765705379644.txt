PROMPT 2 — CHAT-BASED ONBOARDING (ANONYMOUS → SIGNUP)

Context:
NutriCore positions the AI as a personal trainer, not a chatbot.
In this step, your task is to implement the chat-based onboarding experience for anonymous users, up to the point where signup is logically required.

Do not change pricing, access states, UI themes, or feature flags defined previously.

⸻

Objective

Create a natural, premium, chat-based onboarding flow that:
	•	Lets anonymous users evaluate the trainer’s intelligence
	•	Feels personal, calm, and science-based
	•	Transitions cleanly to signup without pressure

⸻

Onboarding Principles (Must Be Followed)
	•	One question at a time
	•	No forms disguised as chat
	•	No feature explanations unless relevant
	•	No marketing language
	•	No emojis
	•	Calm, confident tone

⸻

Anonymous Chat Flow Requirements

1. Opening Message (Trainer Initiates)
The AI trainer must open with a single, open-ended question, such as:
	•	“What are you trying to improve right now?”

No introductions. No credentials. No app explanations.

⸻

2. Question Scope (Strict Limit)
During anonymous onboarding, the trainer may ask only 2–3 high-signal questions, selected from:
	•	Primary goal
	•	Preferred activities or sports
	•	Time availability

Restrictions:
	•	Do NOT ask for age, weight, height, or medical data
	•	Do NOT ask nutrition details yet

⸻

3. Reflection After Each Answer
After each user response, the trainer must briefly summarize or reflect before moving on.

Example behavior:
	•	“Got it — fat loss with limited weekly time.”

This is required to signal listening and memory intent (even though memory is not yet enabled).

⸻

4. Competence Signal (Subtle, Mandatory)
By the second or third exchange, the trainer must include one calm insight, such as:
	•	“Given your schedule, consistency will matter more than intensity.”

No citations. No buzzwords.

⸻

5. Limitation Transparency (Soft)
Before the user reaches the 50-message limit, the trainer must state calmly:
	•	That memory and full personalization require an account
	•	That nothing will be saved unless they sign up

This must feel informational, not restrictive.

⸻

Signup Transition (Critical)

When the user requests:
	•	A plan
	•	Deeper personalization
	•	Saving progress

The trainer must respond with logic, not sales:

“To build this properly and keep it consistent, I’ll need to save your preferences.”

Then trigger the signup prompt.

⸻

Constraints
	•	Do NOT mention pricing
	•	Do NOT mention trials
	•	Do NOT mention subscriptions
	•	Do NOT unlock any paid features yet

⸻

Output Expected
	•	Defined chat flow logic for anonymous onboarding
	•	Trigger points for signup
	•	Guardrails to prevent over-questioning
	•	Consistent tone enforcement

⸻

Important
	•	This step must work independently of payment logic
	•	Do not bundle post-signup behavior here
